Goal: use generated labels to turn the problem into weakly supervised training.

Do this first:
1. Choose one pseudo-label generator and make the pipeline deterministic.
2. Filter out obviously bad masks before training.
3. Train a standard segmentation model on the pseudo-labels.

Then:
1. Compare pseudo-label quality against true labels on validation data.
2. Refine the pseudo-labels if the model learns obvious artifacts.
3. Only add contour losses after the pseudo-label baseline is working.

Do not:
1. Assume pseudo-labels are correct without checking them.
2. Mix too many pseudo-label sources at once.
3. Let the label-generation step become a one-off manual process.
