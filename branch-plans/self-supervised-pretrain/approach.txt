Goal: improve the encoder first, then attach segmentation.

Do this first:
1. Pick a self-supervised objective that is practical to run here.
2. Pretrain the encoder on Kvasir images or a broader unlabeled image pool.
3. Fine-tune the pretrained encoder with a supervised segmentation head.

Then:
1. Measure whether pretraining improves Dice, IoU, or convergence speed.
2. Compare against the same architecture trained from scratch.
3. Keep the pretraining setup simple enough to reproduce.

Do not:
1. Overcomplicate the pretraining recipe.
2. Change the segmentation head and the pretraining objective at the same time.
3. Claim success unless it improves the downstream segmentation task.
